Hierarchical Reinforcement Learning with Unsupervised Skill Discovery for Autonomous Driving

1Bilkent University

Abstract

Despite years of work by technology companies and universities, autonomous driving is still a difficult problem to solve. Although some countries allow the use of autonomous vehicles in designated areas, this technology has not yet been widely accepted. One of the main reasons for this is the wide observation and decision space in which control algorithms operate. Deep reinforcement learning is one of the most promising candidates for solving this problem.

However, one of the weakest aspects of reinforcement learning methods is the need for a large number of iterations for learning. If the learning environment has sparse rewards — as is common in traffic — the number of required iterations increases even further. Another solution to the sparse reward problem is hierarchical reinforcement learning (HRL), where actions are policies. Lower-level policies are trained first with dense rewards independent of higher-level goals; then a higher-level policy learns how to select among them.

The contribution of this project is the development of an autonomous driving control framework based on unsupervised skill policies. Unlike human-designed policies, these skills are developed without human intervention, completely automatically, eliminating human bias. This enables the development of an autonomous driving algorithm that can be trained faster, achieves high performance, and behaves similarly to human drivers due to its hierarchical structure.
